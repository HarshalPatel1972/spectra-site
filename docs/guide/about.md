# Design Manifesto

**Cryptographic risk has always been invisible.**

Not because it's complex. Not because it's hidden. 
Because nobody built a way to see it.

For decades, RSA was the correct choice. SHA-1 was the right answer. 
The documentation said so. The libraries defaulted to it. Your colleagues used it. 
And it worked.

Then the world changed.

Not yet, you say. Quantum computers aren't powerful enough. 
True. And it takes three to five years to migrate an enterprise cryptographic infrastructure. 
You do the math.

Spectra exists because the distance between *not yet* and *too late* is shorter 
than most migration timelines.

We built a precision instrument that answers the question everyone has been avoiding: 
*"What cryptography am I actually using, where exactly, and what does it cost to change it?"*

Not a dashboard. Not a report. 
A map. A precise, evidence-based, standards-aligned map of your cryptographic landscape — 
so that when the decision comes, and it will, you already know the path.

We believe measurement is the first act of intelligence. 
We believe every finding deserves a source citation. 
We believe migration plans should be ordered, not overwhelming. 
We believe security tools should treat engineers as intelligent adults. 
We believe "open source" and "enterprise-grade" are not opposing ideas.

*Spectra: See every cipher. Own your migration.*
